A brand book for file-backed delivery work.

Delano is an agent-agnostic delivery runtime with a local viewer for `.project` contracts. These guidelines describe how the identity stays present without performing: warm paper, ledger ink, and one forest mark that behaves like a source seal.

§ 02Overview

The Local Dossier

Delano should feel like a precise local dossier: warm paper, clear indexes, compact annotations, and no performance of importance. The product is the delivery state on disk: specs, plans, workstreams, tasks, decisions, updates, and evidence. The brand mark brings an organic forest shape into an otherwise restrained operational surface; it should act like a stamp or source seal: present, recognizable, and quiet.

Key characteristics
Posture
Quiet, document-like, deliberate. The interface is a place to read, not a place to be impressed.
Neutrals
Warm paper surfaces with tinted ink. Never pure black, never pure white.
Identity
The forest mark is identity, not decoration. Use it where presence matters; leave it out where it would compete.
Accent
Slate appears for selection and workflow signal only. Under 10% of any screen.
Hierarchy
Flat — created by borders, spacing, sticky navigation, and typography. Not by shadow or chrome.
Provenance
Source paths, timestamps, and read-only state stay visible. Trust is shown, not implied.
§ 03Logo system

Three surfaces, one mark

The horizontal lockup carries broad placements. The standalone mark sits inside the app. The circular favicon appears wherever the browser or operating system insists on a contained shape. SVG is preferred wherever the renderer supports it.

Clear space

For the horizontal lockup, reserve at least the height of the lowercase letter body around the mark. For the standalone mark, reserve at least 20 percent of its width on every side. In the sidebar, the mark may sit inside the existing 26 px control rhythm without a decorative container.

Color rules

Four lines that resolve almost every palette question before it becomes a meeting.

Rule 01

The Contract Ink Rule

Text and source structure carry the interface. Color is evidence, state, selection, or brand presence — never ornament.

Rule 02

The Two-Green Rule

Product success green and brand forest green serve different jobs. Success green reports validation. Brand forest identifies Delano. Do not use brand forest to mean success.

Rule 03

The One Accent Rule

Slate accent appears on less than 10 percent of a screen. If it is visible everywhere, it has lost its meaning.

Rule 04

The Warm Paper Rule

The viewer never uses pure white or pure black. All neutrals stay slightly warm and tinted.

Typography rules

Rule 01

The Reading Surface Rule

Document body text gets more line height than product chrome. The UI is compact; project contracts are comfortable to read.

Rule 02

The Mono Evidence Rule

Use monospace only where exact provenance matters. Do not use it as a decorative technology cue.

Rule 03

The Asset Type Rule

If the Delano name needs the Red Hat Display shape, use delano-logo.svg. If text needs to be selectable, searchable, or responsive, use Inter.

Rule 04

The Wordmark Boundary

Red Hat Display is logo artwork. Do not use it in any other context.

§ 06Spacing & radius

Quiet rhythm, square corners

A small spacing scale and a small radius scale keep the interface deliberate. Most edges are 4 px; only chips and avatars use the pill. The 48 px page gutter sets the desktop content rhythm.

Layout rules

Rule 01

The Ledger Row Rule

When information is naturally tabular, use rows, dividers, and labels. Do not turn every item into a card.

Rule 02

The Source Stays Visible Rule

Paths, timestamps, read-only state, and generated-at metadata remain visible near the content they describe.

Rule 03

The Open Prose Rule

Markdown prose should not be boxed unless it is code, frontmatter, a table, or a deliberately framed metadata block.

Rule 04

The Flat Ledger Rule

Surfaces are separated by line, tone, and position. Shadows are prohibited unless a future interactive overlay genuinely needs spatial separation.

§ 08Motion

Brief, functional, never decorative

Three durations cover everything. Layout properties are never animated, and there is no page-load choreography. The system prefers exponential exit curves — ease-out-quart, ease-out-quint — so feedback resolves quickly and leaves the page alone.

Durations
90ms
Hover feedback
Tone, border, and text-color shifts inside dense controls. Quiet enough to register as confirmation, not animation.
150–250ms
State changes
Reveals, loading transitions, and selection state. Ease-out-quart or ease-out-quint; never a bounce or overshoot.
0ms
Decoration
No animated hero, no page-load sequence, no looping micro-interaction. The page is a document; it does not greet you.
§ 09Do & do not

Boundaries of the brand

The list on the left is what Delano looks like working correctly. The list on the right is what dissolves the brand instantly. Both columns are short and intentional; if a new rule is needed often, the system itself probably needs to change first.

Affirm / refuse

Do

Keep the dossier intact
  • Use the OKLCH tokens from the viewer CSS as the canonical product palette.
  • Prefer SVG logo assets from docs/images wherever the renderer supports it.
  • Keep Red Hat Display confined to the supplied wordmark asset.
  • Use Inter for display, headings, body, labels, and buttons.
  • Preserve source paths, timestamps, generated-at labels, and read-only cues as visible provenance.
  • Separate dense information with hairline borders and varied spacing instead of stacked cards.
  • Make project contracts comfortable to read: 15 px document body, 1.65 line height, 65–75 characters per line.
  • Use labels and dots together for status — never color alone.

Do not

Keep the chrome out of the way
  • Do not make Delano flashy, decorative, or marketing-led.
  • Do not use generic SaaS tropes — hero metrics, promotional landing-page composition, ornamental UI inside the product surface.
  • Do not use playful mascot agents, heavy enterprise PM-suite density, or dark cyber-terminal styling.
  • Do not add gradient text, decorative glassmorphism, colored side-stripe borders, or identical icon-card grids.
  • Do not turn every repeated item into a card. Tables, lists, and summary strips are first-class structures.
  • Do not use slate accent as decoration. It is for selection and workflow signal only.
  • Do not use brand forest or mint as generic UI decoration.
  • Do not animate layout properties or add page-load choreography.
